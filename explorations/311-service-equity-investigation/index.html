Methodology

This analysis combines 1,084,488 Philadelphia 311 service requests from 2023-2024 with US Census Bureau American Community Survey (ACS) 5-Year Estimates for income and poverty data.

Data Sources:

  • 311 Service Requests: OpenDataPhilly CARTO API
  • Census Income Data: US Census Bureau ACS 2022 (B19013_001E, B17001)
  • Geographic Boundaries: Philadelphia census tracts (384 tracts)

Statistical Analysis:

  • Spearman rank correlation: ρ = -0.3379, p < 0.000001
  • Negative correlation indicates lower income neighborhoods experience slower service
  • P-value < 0.000001 indicates less than 0.0001% chance of random occurrence
  • 363 census tracts analyzed with complete data (≥10 requests per tract)

Key Finding:

Residents in the lowest-income census tracts (bottom 20%, median income $27,112) wait 1,095 hours (45.6 days) for service, while residents in the highest-income tracts (top 20%, median income $102,969) wait 570 hours (23.8 days) — a gap of 525 hours (21.9 days).
